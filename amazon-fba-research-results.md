### Part 5: Understanding Amazon Policies \& Restrictions

#### Gated Categories to Avoid as a Beginner

Amazon restricts certain categories to combat counterfeiting and ensure quality. Starting here is nearly impossible without brand authorization:[^16]


| Category | Why Gated | Requirement |
| :-- | :-- | :-- |
| **Baby Products** | Safety/liability | Invoices from authorized wholesalers |
| **Beauty \& Personal Care** | Authenticity | Invoices, sometimes brand approval |
| **Dietary Supplements** | FDA compliance | invoices, GMP certification |
| **Jewelry** | Counterfeits | Invoices, sometimes category approval |
| **Watches** | Counterfeits | Invoices, brand authorization |
| **Electronics** | Returns/safety | Invoices, brand approval for major brands |
| **Luxury Brands** | IP protection | Brand authorization (Nike, Apple, etc.) |

**Recommendation:** Stick to "ungated" categories for your first 30 days: Home \& Kitchen, Sports \& Outdoors, Arts \& Crafts, Toys \& Games, Office Products, Pet Supplies (non-food).[^17][^16]

#### Brand Restrictions (Don't Sell These Without Authorization)

Nike, Apple, Adidas, Samsung, Under Armour, Canon, Nikon, Sony, Dyson, and luxury fashion brands are heavily restricted. Attempting to sell unauthorized versions results in account suspension.[^18][^19]

**Safe Strategy:** Sell under your own brand name, use private label/white-label suppliers, or stick to truly generic products (like storage containers, organizational items, accessories).[^16]

#### Amazon's Dropshipping Policy

Amazon explicitly prohibits traditional dropshipping‚Äîpurchasing from retailers and having items shipped directly to customers with the retailer's name on the invoice. This violates Amazon's requirement that sellers be the "seller of record."[^12][^20]

**What IS Allowed:**

- POD (you control design/branding)
- Online Arbitrage (you take possession, then ship to FBA)
- Retail Arbitrage (you personally inspect, then send to FBA)
- Legitimate wholesale (you're authorized distributor)

**What Violates Policy:**

- Dropshipping from Oberlo, AliExpress, or similar to customer
- Using supplier's invoice/branding instead of your own
- Not disclosing country of origin

### Part 9: PPC Advertising Fundamentals

#### Why PPC Matters for New Listings

When your product is brand new, it has no rank, no reviews, and no sales history. Amazon's algorithm deprioritizes new listings. PPC (Pay-Per-Click) advertising bypasses this by putting your product directly in front of shoppers searching for relevant keywords.[^25][^26]

**Direct Impact:** PPC drives your first 10‚Äì50 sales, which generates initial reviews and sales velocity signals that help organic ranking.[^26]

#### Setting Up Your First Campaign

**Step 1: Navigate to Advertising**
Seller Central ‚Üí Advertising ‚Üí Campaign Manager

**Step 2: Choose Campaign Type**
For beginners, start with **Sponsored Products** (simplest and most controllable).

**Step 3: Configure Campaign Settings**


| Setting | Beginner Value |
| :-- | :-- |
| Campaign Name | "Q4 2025 ‚Äì Fuzzy Socks ‚Äì Auto" |
| Daily Budget | \$10‚Äì20 (test budget) |
| Start Date | Immediate |
| End Date | Leave blank (ongoing) |
| Bidding Strategy | "Dynamic bids ‚Äì up and down" (Amazon adjusts based on conversion likelihood) |

**Step 4: Choose Targeting Type**

- **Automatic Targeting** (recommended for first 2 weeks)
    - Amazon automatically selects keywords and matches based on your listing
    - Great for discovering keyword insights
    - Lower control but less setup
- **Manual Targeting** (after gathering initial data)
    - You select specific keywords
    - Higher control but requires keyword research

**Step 5: Set Keywords (Manual Targeting)**
Start with 10‚Äì15 keywords that match your product intent:

- Broad keywords (fuzzy socks)
- Exact long-tail (cozy socks non-slip)
- Related terms (warm socks, winter socks)

**Step 6: Set Bids**

- **Starting bid:** Use Amazon's suggested bid (\$1.50‚Äì2.50 for most categories)
- **Adjust by keyword:** Bid higher on high-intent keywords, lower on broad/exploratory ones
- **Rule of thumb:** Your bid should be 10‚Äì20% of your product margin

Example: If your net margin is \$5 per sale, bid between \$0.50‚Äì1.00 initially.[^27][^25][^26]

**Step 7: Review \& Launch**

- Double-check product selection
- Verify targeting type and keywords
- Confirm budget and bid amounts
- Click "Launch Campaign"


#### Monitoring \& Optimization (Days 1‚Äì14)

Check your campaign daily:

- **Impressions:** Number of times your ad was shown (target: 50+ per day initially)
- **Clicks:** Number of clicks (aim for 0.5‚Äì2% click-through rate)
- **Spend:** Is it staying under your daily budget?
- **Sales/Conversions:** Target 1‚Äì2 sales per day initially

**Week 2 Adjustments:**

1. Pause keywords with >\$2 spend but zero sales
2. Increase bids on keywords driving sales
3. Add new negative keywords (e.g., "cheap," "discount" if you're premium-positioning)
4. Reduce bid on broad-match keywords if converting poorly

**Key Metric to Track:** **ACoS (Advertising Cost of Sales)**
ACoS = (Ad Spend √∑ Ad Sales Revenue) √ó 100

Example: If you spent \$50 in ads and made \$250 in sales, your ACoS is 20%. Target: 20‚Äì35% ACoS for sustainable profitability.[^27]

### Part 10: Avoiding Policy Violations \& Account Suspension

#### Red Flags That Trigger Account Review

1. **Sudden spike in returns or negative reviews** ‚Äì Amazon flags accounts with >3% return rates or unusual review patterns
2. **Selling restricted/gated products without approval** ‚Äì Immediate suspension
3. **Using supplier's branding/invoice (dropshipping)** ‚Äì Policy violation
4. **Price gouging or manipulation** ‚Äì Sudden 100%+ price increases trigger reviews
5. **Policy-violating product descriptions** ‚Äì Health claims (e.g., "cures anxiety"), prohibited items
6. **Hijacked listings** ‚Äì Selling on ASIN you don't own (common beginner mistake)

#### Protecting Your Account

- **Use your own branding** ‚Äì Your business name on all invoices/packaging
- **Monitor your listings** ‚Äì Use Helium 10's "Listing Guard" to detect unauthorized hijackers
- **Maintain quality standards** ‚Äì Ship on time, respond to messages within 24 hours
- **Avoid questionable sourcing** ‚Äì Don't buy from unlicensed sellers or counterfeit sources
- **Document everything** ‚Äì Keep invoices, receipts, supplier communications


#### If Suspended

Amazon typically gives you 7 days to appeal. Your response must:

1. Acknowledge the specific policy violation
2. Explain how it happened (if accidental)
3. Detail corrective steps you've taken
4. Provide supporting documentation (invoices, supplier contracts, etc.)

Most first-time violations result in account reinstatement if you respond professionally and thoroughly.

### Part 14: Key Warnings \& Common Mistakes

**‚ùå Mistake \#1: Skipping Validation**
Don't trust your gut. Use data. Products that seem obvious (phone chargers, basic t-shirts) are usually saturated.[^15]

**‚ùå Mistake \#2: Underestimating Amazon Fees**
Beginners often calculate 50% margin but forget referral fees, storage, and advertising. Real margins are 20‚Äì35% for most categories. Use fee calculators religiously.[^9]

**‚ùå Mistake \#3: Starting with Dropshipping from AliExpress**
Amazon's policy explicitly forbids this. Your account will be suspended. Use authorized suppliers or stick to arbitrage/POD.[^12]

**‚ùå Mistake \#4: Selling Gated Products Without Approval**
Beauty, supplements, jewelry, baby products require authorization. Selling without it = immediate suspension.[^16]

**‚ùå Mistake \#5: Neglecting Customer Service**
Response time to messages, handling returns professionally, and addressing negative reviews directly impact your seller metrics and ranking.[^24]

**‚ùå Mistake \#6: Overspending on Inventory Too Early**
Test with 10‚Äì50 units first. Only scale to 100‚Äì300 units after confirming consistent sales and 4+ star reviews.[^8]

**‚ùå Mistake \#7: Ignoring PPC Data**
Many beginners set-and-forget PPC campaigns. Monitor weekly, pause losers, and scale winners. This 10-minute weekly check can double your profitability.[^25]
